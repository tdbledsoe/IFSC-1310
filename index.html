Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Reading over the article on Progressive Enhancement it shows how creating a strong foundation that everyone can access is key to ensuring everyone has access to the content. The old idea of Graceful Degradation limits individuals who might not be able to run the newest frameworks and limits their access to content. The idea of creating a strong foundation not only helps the user but also the developer for longevity. Moving away from Graceful Degradation will help ensure all users have accessibility and will make it so the developer does not have to come back and make contistent updates. While the Progressive Enhancement model does require more forethought it, ensuring the user has at least access to the content should be the end goal. More information can be found at freeCodeCamp where they explain in more detail the pros of the Progressive Enhancement model and how the Graceful Degradation is obsolete.
